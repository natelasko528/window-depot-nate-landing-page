## 3. Common Patterns Across Competitor Websites

### Structural & UX
- **Hero + CTA:** Large hero with tagline and primary CTA (quote, schedule, free estimate)
- **Multi-step process:** 3–4 steps (consultation → measure → install → warranty)
- **Product categories:** Windows, doors, siding, roofing in clear nav
- **Trust signals:** BBB, reviews, certifications, years in business
- **Lead forms:** Name, email, phone, address, product interest

### Messaging
- **Free estimate / consultation** — universal
- **Financing** — 0% or no payments for X months
- **Warranty** — lifetime or long-term
- **Local + national** — “local service” + “national strength” or “trusted brand”
- **Energy efficiency** — ENERGY STAR, insulation, savings

### Visual
- **Photography:** Product shots, before/after, happy homeowners
- **Colors:** Blues, greens, whites; professional, trustworthy
- **Mobile-first:** Responsive layouts
- **CTAs:** “Get a Quote,” “Schedule Consultation,” “Free Estimate”

### Content
- **Blogs:** Wisconsin/local content, FAQs, how-to
- **Testimonials:** Throughout site
- **Showroom info:** Address, hours, virtual tours where offered

## 4. Gaps & Opportunities

### Gaps (Where Competitors Lead)
1. **Premium material story:** RBA’s Fibrex narrative is strong; Window Depot’s triple-pane story is less differentiated in copy.
2. **Pricing transparency:** Zen’s “no in-home sales, transparent quote” is unique; Window Depot doesn’t emphasize this.
3. **Ownership story:** All American (female-owned), Weather Tight (employee-owned) stand out; Window Depot’s family-owned angle could be stronger.
4. **Certifications:** Exterior Pros’ GAF President’s Club and Angi awards are prominent; Window Depot could surface similar credentials more.
5. **Interactive tools:** Some national brands use configurators, quizzes; Window Depot’s site is more traditional.

### Opportunities for Window Depot
1. **Triple-pane at dual-pane price** — Clear, repeatable headline; most competitors don’t lead with this.
2. **7 showrooms** — Strong local footprint; highlight locations and “visit in person” more.
3. **Full-service breadth** — Windows, doors, siding, roofing, flooring, bathrooms; few competitors offer all six.
4. **$500 gift card** — Distinct promotion; ensure it’s visible on site and ads.
5. **Nate Lasko personal brand** — Personal rep/owner landing page (wdusa-nate-landing.vercel.app) is a differentiator; competitors rarely personalize to this degree.
6. **ProVia alignment** — Same brand as Exterior Pros; can match premium positioning while emphasizing price and local service.
7. **Review volume** — 840+ reviews and 4.9 stars; use more prominently in hero and trust sections.
8. **Bath Makeover 1-day install** — Unique; can be a secondary hero offer.

### Competitive Threats
- **RBA:** Premium brand and Fibrex; targets higher-end buyers.
- **Zen:** Appeals to price-sensitive, no-pressure buyers.
- **Feldco:** Scale, trust badges, multi-state presence.
- **Exterior Pros:** Same ProVia triple-pane + GAF roofing; direct product overlap.
